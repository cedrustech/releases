MME - Marhati Maharashtra Emporium

    Popularly known as "Marhati Emporium". The MME is a Government of Maharashtra Undertaking project, where the artisans can sell their products through the Emporia where they can either hire their own sales counter or MME provides space for selling on commission basis. The artisans are required to pay a fixed amount of commission on the total sale of the articles during the particular month. How Cedrus Tech Helps the Marhati Emporium

    The MME ("Marhati Emporium") is responsible for providing essential services to their customers and consignments. They faced the daunting task of managing their bills, keeping tabs on their stocks, maintaining offline reports and handling invoices efficiently. The paperwork had become overwhelming, and errors were creeping in, causing delays and frustration.

    Cedrus Tech embarked on a mission to transform the operations of this Emporium. We knew that Salesforce could be the game-changer they needed.

    Cedrus Tech provided the ultimate solution - the MME Project. This groundbreaking app was designed to streamline the emporium's billing process, manage their stock information, and provide real-time insights through reports and dashboards.

With MME Project, the Marhati Emporium could:

  1. Effortlessly Calculate Bills: The days of manual bill calculations were gone. The app automated the entire billing process, ensuring accuracy and reducing the risk of errors.

  2. Stock Management Simplified: Tracking stock was no longer a headache. The app allowed them to monitor how many items were sold, what was available, and when they needed to restock.

  3. Invoice Control: Creating, managing, and tracking invoices became a breeze. Everything was organized in one place, saving time and reducing confusion.

  4. Insightful Reports and Dashboards: Thanks to the app's robust reporting and dashboard capabilities, the company could access real-time data, enabling them to make informed decisions and optimize their operations.

    Productivity soared, errors plummeted, and the employees were no longer bogged down by paperwork. The Emporium's management could now make data-driven decisions to improve their services, reduce costs, and allocate resources more efficiently.
